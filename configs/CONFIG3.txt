NON-TECHNICAL DEMONSTRATION WALKTHROUGH

Project: Greenfield Hub - Local Produce Online Shop
Audience: General Users / Customers (No Technical Background Required)

PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT

This document walks through the Greenfield Hub website from a user's point of
view. It is written for people who have not used the site before and explains
what each part of the website does and how to use it.

No technical knowledge is needed to follow this guide.

WHAT IS GREENFIELD HUB?

Greenfield Hub is an online shop where you can browse and buy fresh, locally
produced food — such as organic tomatoes, free-range eggs, strawberries, and
honey — directly from local farms and producers.

The site allows you to:

Browse and search for products
Create an account and log in
Place orders for products you want to buy
View information about the producers behind the food
HOW TO USE THE WEBSITE – STEP BY STEP
STEP 1: The Home Page

When you first visit Greenfield Hub, you land on the home page.
Here you will see:

A welcome banner and a short description of the site
A selection of featured products available to buy
A navigation bar at the top with links to: Home, Shop, Producers, FAQ

What to do: Have a look at the featured products. You can click on any
product to find out more about it.

STEP 2: Browsing the Shop (Catalogue)

Click "Shop" in the top menu to go to the full product catalogue.
Here you can see all available products, including:

The product name
The price per unit
How many are currently in stock

You can also use the search bar to find a specific product by name.
For example: typing "honey" will show only honey-related products.

STEP 3: Viewing a Product

Click on any product to see its individual page.
This shows you more detail about that product and gives you the option
to place an order.

STEP 4: Placing an Order

On a product's page, you can choose how many you want to buy and click
the order button.

The website will check that:

You have entered a valid quantity (more than zero)
There is enough stock available

If successful, you will see a confirmation message and the stock level
will automatically update.

If there is not enough stock, the site will let you know.

STEP 5: Creating an Account / Logging In

To use the full features of the site, you can create an account by
clicking "Register" in the navigation bar.

You will be asked for:

Your email address
A password

Once registered, you can log in at any time using those details.
The site will remember who you are while you are browsing.

To log out, click "Logout" in the navigation bar.

STEP 6: Producers Page

Click "Producers" in the top menu to learn more about the local farms
and producers that supply the products on the site.
This page includes photos and information about each producer.

STEP 7: FAQ Page

If you have any questions about how the site works, the FAQ page
(Frequently Asked Questions) has answers to common queries.

THINGS TO LOOK OUT FOR DURING YOUR REVIEW

As you explore the site, please consider:

Was it easy to find what you were looking for?
Did the site feel welcoming and easy to understand?
Were there any moments where you felt confused or stuck?
Did the ordering process feel straightforward?
Did the site look good and feel trustworthy?
Is there anything you would change or add?

Your honest feedback will help improve the site for future users.
Thank you for your time!
